By subjecting the organic solid waste from the FIRE module to **Anaerobic Slow Pyrolysis** ($400-600^\circ\text{C}$), we strip away volatile compounds while fusing the remaining carbon atoms into a highly recalcitrant graphene-like matrix (O:C ratio $<0.2$). The decay constant of this material approaches zero, ensuring $>1000$ years of sequestration in the soil.

To achieve this zero-oxygen baking process without utilizing external fossil-fuel energy, we leverage a thermodynamic trick: the outgassing syngas ($H_2$ + $CO$) from the baking biomass is routed back into the firebox to sustain the endothermic reaction. This requires a specific hardware design known as a Double-Barrel Retort.
