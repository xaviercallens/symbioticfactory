### Chemicals
1. **Sodium Tungstate Dihydrate ($Na_2WO_4 \cdot 2H_2O$):** Precursor for the semiconductor lattice. (Readily available online).
2. **Hydrochloric Acid (HCl):** $3$ Molar concentration. Used to precipitate the tungstic acid.
3. **Silver Nitrate ($AgNO_3$):** Reagent grade ($>99\%$). The Z-scheme bridge.
4. **Methanol ($CH_3OH$):** Acts as a sacrificial hole scavenger during the photodeposition step.
5. **Nafion Solution (or dilute PVA):** Used as a binder to paint the catalyst onto the glass.
6. **Deionized (DI) Water.**

### Equipment
- Magnetic stirrer with hot plate
- Precision scale ($\pm 0.01\text{g}$)
- Standard glass beakers
- Centrifuge (or coffee filters and extreme patience for gravity filtration)
- UV Lamp (365nm)
- A large, clear glass reactor (e.g., a $1\text{L}$ wide-mouth mason jar)

## ðŸ”¬ Protocol Steps

### Step 1: Precipitation of the $WO_3$ Semiconductor Matrix
1. Dissolve $3.3\text{g}$ of Sodium Tungstate in $100\text{mL}$ of DI water. Stir vigorously on the magnetic stirrer until completely clear.
2. **Fume Hood / Outdoors:** Slowly add $3\text{M}$ HCl drop-by-drop into the stirring solution. You will see a pale yellow precipitate begin to form (this is turning the tungstate into tungstic acid/hydrate).
3. Keep adjusting the pH with HCl until it reaches approximately **pH 1.0**.
4. Heat the stirring beaker to $90^\circ\text{C}$ for 4 hours. The yellow precipitate will deepen in color.
5. Turn off heat. Allow the yellow $WO_3$ powder to settle. 
6. Wash the powder 3 times: Decant the liquid, add fresh DI water, stir, and let settle (or centrifuge at $4000\text{ RPM}$). For the final wash, use Methanol to help it dry faster.
7. Dry the yellow powder in an oven at $80^\circ\text{C}$ overnight.

### Step 2: Z-Scheme Photodeposition of Silver (Ag) Nanoparticles
1. Take $1.0\text{g}$ of your dry yellow $WO_3$ powder and disperse it into $100\text{mL}$ of DI water mixed with $10\text{mL}$ of Methanol. (The Methanol will "eat" the positive holes generated by the UV light, allowing the electrons to be totally dedicated to reducing the silver).
2. Stir vigorously in the dark for 30 minutes to establish an adsorption equilibrium.
3. In the dark, add $0.05\text{g}$ of Silver Nitrate ($AgNO_3$) to the stirring suspension.
4. **The Z-Scheme Creation:** Turn on the UV lamp and irradiate the stirring beaker directly for 1 to 2 hours. The suspension will darken slightly (shifting from pure yellow to a greyish-yellow) as nanoscale Silver precipitates perfectly onto the $WO_3$ crystal facets.
5. Wash, centrifuge/filter, and dry the greyish-yellow $Ag/WO_3$ powder at $80^\circ\text{C}$ overnight.

### Step 3: Reactor Painting (Glass Coating)
1. Mix $0.5\text{g}$ of your synthesized $Ag/WO_3$ powder with $5\text{mL}$ of DI water and $2$ drops of Nafion solution (or a few drops of the PVA hydrogel from the SUN module). Sonicate or stir wildly until it forms a uniform "paint."
2. Take your clean, sterilized glass reactor (mason jar).
3. Using a fine brush or a spin-coating method, paint the *interior walls* of the jar with the catalyst slurry. Leave a "window" clear on one side so you can observe the interior later if needed.
4. Bake the jar in an oven at $100^\circ\text{C}$ for 2 hours to firmly cure the binder and adhere the catalyst to the glass.
