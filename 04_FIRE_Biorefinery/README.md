The FIRE module actually operates in two strictly separated streams to maximize carbon capture:
1. **The Solid/Lipid Stream (HTL):** Wet algae is crushed under immense pressure ($\approx 150\text{ bar}$) and heat ($300^\circ\text{C}$). The resulting extracted heavy bio-crude serves as a drop-in petroleum replacement.
2. **The Gas Stream (Z-Scheme Symbiosis):** The WATER module isn't 100% efficient. Unused $CO_2$ that bubbles out of the top of the cycloreactors is routed into a specialized glass jar coated in a **synthesized $Ag/WO_3$ Z-Scheme Photocatalyst**. Under sunlight, this catalyst strips oxygen from the $CO_2$, converting it into **Carbon Monoxide (CO)**. This CO is bubbled into a dark fermentation tank where extremophile bacteria (*Clostridium autoethanogenum*) ingest the toxic gas and excrete pure Ethanol or Butanol via the Wood-Ljungdahl metabolic pathway.
