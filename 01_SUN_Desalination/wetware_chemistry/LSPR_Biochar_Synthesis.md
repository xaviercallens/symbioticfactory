### Chemicals
1. **Raw Biochar (PAC):** Harvested from Module III (TERRE). Must be highly porous.
2. **PVA (Polyvinyl Alcohol) Powder:** ~$99\%$ hydrolyzed, molecular weight ~$89,000-98,000$. Acts as the hydrophilic polymer binder.
3. **Silver Nitrate ($AgNO_3$):** Reagent grade ($>99\%$). This is the plasmonic precursor.
4. **Sodium Citrate Crystalline:** Acts as a mild reducing agent and capping agent to stabilize the nanoparticles.
5. **Deionized (DI) Water:** Do not use tap water; the chlorine will precipitate the Silver Nitrate into useless Silver Chloride ($AgCl$).

### Equipment
- Magnetic stirrer with hot plate
- Coffee grinder or mortar & pestle
- Precision scale ($\pm 0.01\text{g}$)
- Standard glass beakers or sterilized mason jars
- UV Lamp (365nm) or direct, intense midday sunlight
- **Drying Tech:** A lyophilizer (Freeze Dryer) is optimal. *See Step 4 for a DIY Hacker alternative.*

## ðŸ”¬ Protocol Steps

### Step 1: Substrate Preparation (Milling)
1. Take $50\text{g}$ of dry PAC biochar from Module III.
2. Mill the biochar using a coffee grinder or mortar until it passes through a 100-mesh ($150\text{ }\mu\text{m}$) sieve. Consistency should be like very fine flour.

### Step 2: The Hydrogel Binder
1. Heat $200\text{mL}$ of DI water to $90^\circ\text{C}$ on the magnetic stirrer.
2. Slowly whisk in $10\text{g}$ of PVA powder. **Do this extremely slowly** to prevent the PVA from clumping.
3. Stir at $300\text{ RPM}$ for 1-2 hours until the solution is completely clear and viscous. Let it cool to room temperature.
4. Fold $20\text{g}$ of the milled biochar into the PVA solution until you achieve a uniform, thick black slurry. 
5. Pour the slurry into shallow molds (e.g., silicone pucks) to a depth of $10\text{-}15\text{mm}$.

### Step 3: Plasmonic Doping ($Ag^+$ to $Ag^0$ Precipitation)
1. **Dark Room Step:** Prepare a $0.05\text{ M}$ Silver Nitrate solution by dissolving $0.85\text{g}$ of $AgNO_3$ in $100\text{mL}$ of DI water. Keep away from bright light.
2. Prepare a separate $0.1\text{ M}$ Sodium Citrate solution ($2.94\text{g}$ in $100\text{mL}$ DI water).
3. Soak the molded biochar/PVA pucks in the $AgNO_3$ solution for 30 minutes in the dark to allow capillary absorption.
4. Transfer the soaked pucks into the Sodium Citrate bath.
5. **The Plasmonic Catalyst:** Immediately expose the bath to a strong UV lamp or direct midday sunlight. You will observe the solution turn slightly yellow/brown as the $Ag^+$ ions are photochemically reduced by the citrate into elemental Ag nanoparticles on the surface of the biochar.
6. Expose for 2 hours. Rinse the pucks gently with DI water.

### Step 4: Curing & Fixing (The Nanoconfinement Lock)
The ultimate goal is to maintain the extreme microscopic porosity of the PVA-Biochar matrix. If you just air-dry the puck, capillary-tension forces will collapse the pores as the water evaporates, destroying its ability to nanoconfine water.

* **Method A (Industrial/Lab):** Freeze the pucks at $-80^\circ\text{C}$, then lyophilize (freeze-dry) under vacuum for 48 hours. The ice sublimates directly to gas, leaving the pore structure intact.
* **Method B (DIY Hacker Alternative):** The Acetone Exchange. 
  1. Submerge the wet puck in a bath of $100\%$ Acetone for 24 hours. The acetone displaces the water. 
  2. Because acetone has a much lower surface tension than water ($\approx 24\text{ mN/m}$ vs $\approx 72\text{ mN/m}$), it will evaporate without collapsing the delicate pore walls. 
  3. Remove and let air dry in a well-ventilated area for 48 hours until all acetone smell is gone.
